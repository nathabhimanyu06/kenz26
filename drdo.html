About DRDO

The Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) is the R&D wing of the Indian Ministry of Defence, tasked with developing advanced technologies and systems for the Indian Armed Forces. Established in 1958, DRDO is at the forefront of creating innovative solutions in defence and national security. With over 50 laboratories spread across India, DRDO is a leader in the development of cutting-edge technologies in the fields of aerospace, electronics, and missile systems, among others.

Key Missions

DRDO plays a crucial role in the advancement of India's defence capabilities through various groundbreaking missions, including the development of long-range missiles, stealth technologies, and unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs). Some of its major missions include:

  • Development of the Agni and Prithvi missile systems
  • Creation of the Tejas Light Combat Aircraft
  • Development of advanced radar systems
  • Autonomous Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) development

Ongoing Projects

DRDO is currently working on several futuristic projects aimed at enhancing the defence capabilities of India. Some of the ongoing projects include:

  • INSAT Series of Satellites: A group of communication satellites to improve defence communication.
  • Advanced Towed Artillery Gun System (ATAGS): A next-generation artillery gun designed for the Indian Army.
  • Future Infantry Combat Vehicle (FICV): A cutting-edge vehicle for the future needs of the Indian Army.
  • Hypersonic Technology Demonstrator Vehicle (HSTDV): Development of hypersonic cruise missiles.
