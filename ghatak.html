About Ghatak UCAV

The Ghatak UCAV (Unmanned Combat Aerial Vehicle) is an Indian project under development by the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO). It is intended to provide a stealthy, cost-effective platform for various combat missions, including precision strikes on critical targets. It is designed to be launched from Indian Air Force (IAF) bases and can be deployed in a range of strategic military operations.

Features

  • Stealth Design: Designed to evade radar detection with a low radar cross-section.
  • Autonomous Operation: Capable of being operated autonomously or remotely controlled.
  • Advanced Payloads: Equipped with sophisticated payloads for surveillance and strike missions.
  • Enhanced Maneuverability: Optimized for precision in high-threat environments.

History

The Ghatak UCAV program was initiated in the mid-2010s as part of India's broader military modernization efforts. The aim was to develop a cost-effective unmanned aerial vehicle that could operate in high-risk combat zones without risking human pilots. Development has involved several stages, including research, design, and prototype testing.
